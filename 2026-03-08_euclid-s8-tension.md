# Euclid DR1 and the S8 Tension

## STUDY

The Euclid space telescope's Data Release 1 (DR1), planned for October 2026. Euclid is an ESA mission mapping billions of galaxies via weak gravitational lensing and galaxy clustering to constrain dark energy and dark matter. DR1 will contain the first cosmology-grade data: weak lensing shear measurements, galaxy clustering, and photometric redshifts across thousands of square degrees.

The key parameter: S8 = σ8 × √(Ωm/0.3), which quantifies how "clumpy" matter is in the universe. Planck CMB measurements (early universe) give S8 ≈ 0.83. Late-universe weak lensing surveys (DES, KiDS, HSC) consistently find S8 ≈ 0.75. This 2–3σ discrepancy — the "S8 tension" — has persisted across multiple independent surveys for nearly a decade.

DES Year 6 results (January 2026) widened the gap. A 2026 review paper (arXiv 2602.12238) catalogues the tension's current status. Euclid DR1 will provide the most precise weak lensing measurement to date.

## CONSENSUS EXPECTATION

The field is split. Optimists expect Euclid's precision to resolve the tension — either confirming the low S8 value (requiring new physics) or revealing systematic errors in previous surveys. Pessimists expect the tension to persist but remain below the 5σ threshold needed for a definitive claim. Most cosmologists expect Euclid to narrow the error bars substantially on the late-universe S8 value. The default expectation: Euclid confirms S8 ≈ 0.76 ± 0.01, sharpening the tension with Planck to 4–5σ, forcing the community to take it seriously as potential new physics.

Our prediction partially aligns with the "tension persists" expectation. What differs is the structural account of *why* it persists and *why* proposed resolutions will fail in specific ways.

## PREDICTION

1. **Euclid DR1 will confirm the low S8 value**, consistent with DES, KiDS, and HSC. The tension with Planck will sharpen to ≥4σ. This is the easy prediction.

2. **The tension will not be attributable to systematic errors** in any single survey. Euclid's independent methodology will rule out the most commonly proposed systematics (photometric redshift errors, intrinsic alignment modelling, baryonic feedback). The field will accept the tension is real.

3. **Proposed resolutions that modify a single sector will each partially work but create new inconsistencies.** Specifically:
   - Models that add dark matter self-interaction will improve S8 but worsen Hubble tension (H0).
   - Models with evolving dark energy (w0waCDM) will improve both tensions slightly but require fine-tuned parameters that don't arise from any physical mechanism.
   - Neutrino-dark matter interaction models (see Nature Astronomy, January 2026) will improve S8 but predict neutrino mass values that conflict with DESI constraints.
   - Modified gravity models will improve S8 at specific redshifts but fail at others.

4. **No single-sector modification will simultaneously resolve S8 tension, Hubble tension, and DESI's evolving dark energy signal.** The pattern will be: each fix helps one tension at the expense of another.

## STRUCTURAL REASONING

ΛCDM treats dark matter, dark energy, and baryonic matter as separable components with independently specifiable properties. The S8 parameter is derived by assuming these components evolve independently according to their own equations of state. The tension arises because this separation fails.

This is a Section 2 error: dark matter, dark energy, and baryonic matter are distinguishable (they have different observational signatures) but not separable (they cannot exist or evolve independently of each other). The universe is one system. Partitioning it into components whose properties are independently specifiable is a modelling choice, not a feature of reality.

The specific prediction — that single-sector modifications create new tensions — follows directly. If the components aren't separable, then modifying one component's properties while holding others fixed will necessarily produce inconsistencies, because the "fixed" components' properties are defined relative to the one you just changed. This is why S8, H0, and dark energy evolution tensions co-occur and resist individual resolution.

## WHAT WOULD DISCONFIRM THIS

- Euclid DR1 finds S8 consistent with Planck (≈0.83), resolving the tension as a systematic error in previous surveys. This would mean the separation works.
- A single-sector modification (e.g., sterile neutrinos, or decaying dark matter) simultaneously resolves S8, H0, and the DESI dark energy signal without creating new tensions. This would mean the components are separable after all — you just had the wrong properties for one of them.
- Euclid DR1 finds a completely unexpected S8 value (significantly below 0.73 or above 0.83), suggesting a measurement-side problem we haven't anticipated.
