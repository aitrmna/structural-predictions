# DESI Dark Degeneracy

## STUDY

The Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI), operated by Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. DESI DR2 (March 2025) mapped 14 million galaxy redshifts and found evidence at ~3.2σ (combined with CMB and supernovae) for evolving dark energy — the equation of state parameter w changing over time rather than remaining constant at w = −1.

The key development: a paper (arXiv 2508.17955, revised January 2026) demonstrated "dark degeneracy" — an interacting dark energy model and the standard evolving dark energy parameterisation (CPL, w0waCDM) produce *identical expansion histories*. Both fit the data significantly better than ΛCDM. The data cannot distinguish between them.

DESI's upcoming full-shape analysis and cross-correlation results (expected 2026) will attempt to break this degeneracy using galaxy clustering information beyond baryon acoustic oscillations.

## CONSENSUS EXPECTATION

The field expects DESI's full-shape analysis to add constraining power. Most cosmologists anticipate that combining BAO with full-shape measurements, redshift-space distortions, and cross-correlations will narrow the parameter space and potentially distinguish between evolving and interacting dark energy. The default expectation: more data will break the degeneracy.

Our prediction differs sharply from this expectation.

## PREDICTION

1. **DESI's full-shape analysis will not break the dark degeneracy.** Evolving dark energy and interacting dark energy models will continue to provide comparably good fits that cannot be statistically distinguished. Additional constraining power from full-shape measurements will tighten parameters *within* each model but will not discriminate *between* them.

2. **Further models will join the degeneracy.** As theorists explore the expanded parameter space enabled by DESI DR2, at least one additional physically distinct model (modified gravity, running vacuum energy, or emergent dark energy) will be shown to produce expansion histories degenerate with the CPL parameterisation at the precision of current data.

3. **The degeneracy will be recognised as deeper than a data problem.** By late 2026 or 2027, papers will explicitly argue that the degeneracy reflects a fundamental limitation of background-level cosmological observations — that expansion history alone cannot distinguish between modifications to the dark sector because the "dark sector" is not a physically separable domain.

## STRUCTURAL REASONING

This is a smuggling error. The question "does dark energy evolve?" smuggles in the assumption that "dark energy" is a separable substance whose equation of state can be independently specified. (This is related to but distinct from a Section 2 error. Section 2 says dark matter and dark energy are distinguished but not separable. The smuggling claim is more specific: the w0wa parameterisation imports a concept — "the equation of state of dark energy" — that presupposes dark energy has its own independent equation of state. This concept was never established; it was assumed by the parameterisation.) If dark energy and dark matter are distinguishable descriptions of a single underlying dynamic — distinguished by their observational signatures but not separable in their physics — then models that modify either one are partial descriptions of the same phenomenon.

The dark degeneracy is the empirical signature of this smuggling. Two models with different physical stories (dark energy changes its properties vs dark energy and dark matter interact) produce identical observations because they are both incomplete descriptions of one thing. Adding more data of the same observational type — galaxy positions, which probe the expansion history and growth of structure — will not break the degeneracy because the degeneracy is not caused by insufficient data. It is caused by the framework's assumption that the dark sector has separable components.

The prediction that the degeneracy is unbreakable by background observations alone follows from the structural analysis: you cannot distinguish between modifications to separable components if the components were never separable. The observations constrain the total system, not its fictional parts.

## WHAT WOULD DISCONFIRM THIS

- DESI full-shape analysis cleanly distinguishes evolving from interacting dark energy at ≥3σ. This would mean the components are observationally separable after all — you just needed the right observable.
- A theoretical argument demonstrates that the degeneracy is exact only at background level but breaks at perturbation level, AND the data confirms the breaking. This would mean the separation holds at a finer grain.
- The w0wa parameterisation converges to w = −1 (cosmological constant) with increased data precision, eliminating both models in favour of ΛCDM. This would dissolve the question rather than answering it — which would be a legitimate outcome that doesn't disconfirm the structural reasoning but does disconfirm prediction #1.
