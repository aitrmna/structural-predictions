# Structural Predictions Portfolio

A portfolio of timestamped, falsifiable predictions across independent scientific domains, all derived from a single structural method. The goal: demonstrate that identifying foundational errors in how questions are framed predicts empirical outcomes across fields that have no connection to each other.

## Method

The method tests whether a scientific debate rests on a structural error — a violated axiom of coherent description. The axioms and methodology are documented in two companion files: *Axioms of Description* (the foundational constraints on coherent description) and *Framework Methodology* (the procedure for comparing competing foundational claims). These will be published separately and linked here when available.

Five types of error generate five types of prediction: **false separations** (treating distinguished things as separable) predict persistent failure to resolve binaries; **identity failures** (treating heterogeneous phenomena as one thing) predict category fracture; **smuggled premises** (conclusions containing concepts never in the premises) predict answers orthogonal to the question; **description/reality conflation** (assuming measurement doesn't affect the measured) predict reflexive saturation; **appearance/reality hierarchies** (dismissing appearances as lesser) predict that "noise" or "error" contains the phenomenon. Each prediction document traces the chain from the specific axiom violation to the specific empirical outcome.
